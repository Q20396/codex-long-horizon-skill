# Experiment Proposal

## Experiment ID
`MAD-SKILL-006`
## Name
Rubric-Blind Failure-to-Procedure Loop
## Status
`locked`
## Problem Statement
Evaluation feedback can improve a procedure only when it does not leak rubrics, answers, task identifiers, or customer facts.
## Why This Is Relevant
A redacted Solver -> Evaluator -> Learner Proposal loop can separate evidence from a future customer promotion decision.
## Source Patterns
Parthenon Law is `verification_blocked`; paper results are `paper_reported_not_locally_reproduced` and no source commit is claimed.
## Clean-room Boundary
Use only the stated abstract pattern; copy no legal workflow, paper content, dataset, or code.
## Explicit Non-goals
No legal advice, law retrieval, client document read, legal task execution, or automatic learning.
## Inputs
Future synthetic non-legal fixture and a customer-approved redaction policy.
## Outputs
Future redacted failure signal and procedure-change proposal.
## Permissions Required
Future isolated synthetic evaluation approval; no customer file access.
## Network Requirements
None for design or future initial synthetic prototype.
## Dependency Requirements
None for design.
## Cost Possibility
Future evaluation may cost compute; none occurs here.
## Customer Approval Gates
Approve fixtures, evaluator boundary, feedback redaction, and every later action separately.
## Expected Benefits
Procedure improvement without retaining customer facts or leaking the scoring rubric.
## Failure Modes
Rubric leakage, memorization, routing leakage, false learning, or regulated-domain misuse.
## Baseline Comparison
Static procedure with no feedback proposal.
## Required Hard Gates
All gates, especially privacy, claim integrity, isolation, and human control.
## Evaluation Categories
Leakage resistance, procedure clarity, reliability, safety, and baseline quality.
## Promotion Constraints
Sandbox-only restricted legal domain; no promotion without independent legal/privacy review and explicit customer approval.
## Rejection Conditions
Reject any client material, task-id routing, answer leakage, or legal advice behavior.
## Open Questions
Can a synthetic redaction contract demonstrate utility without reproducing a legal benchmark?
