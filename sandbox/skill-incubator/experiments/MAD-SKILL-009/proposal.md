# Experiment Proposal

## Experiment ID
`MAD-SKILL-009`
## Name
Decision-to-Diagram
## Status
`locked`
## Problem Statement
Abstract decisions are hard to review when their actors, states, and dependencies are only prose.
## Why This Is Relevant
Mermaid, Graphviz, ASCII, node-edge lists, and graphic requirements can make a future decision model inspectable without image generation.
## Source Patterns
`source_status: rejected_pending_verification`; `design_origin: customer_provided_abstract_pattern`; no third-party commit is claimed.
## Clean-room Boundary
Do not name, cite, imitate, or copy an unverified author or hand-drawn source.
## Explicit Non-goals
No image generation, external source search, visual provider, OCR, or publishing.
## Inputs
Future customer-approved abstract decision structure.
## Outputs
Future text diagram and node-edge specification.
## Permissions Required
Future exact-input approval only; no execution permission.
## Network Requirements
None.
## Dependency Requirements
None.
## Cost Possibility
No cost in design; future rendering tools require a separate decision.
## Customer Approval Gates
Approve input scope, output format, and any later rendering separately.
## Expected Benefits
More reviewable decisions without unverified source or media dependency.
## Failure Modes
Misleading diagram, private content exposure, accidental provider use, or false attribution.
## Baseline Comparison
Prose-only decision explanation.
## Required Hard Gates
All gates; source, scope, privacy, and claim integrity are critical.
## Evaluation Categories
Diagram clarity, factual fidelity, source honesty, safety, and baseline utility.
## Promotion Constraints
Remain sandbox-only until the source is independently verified or treated only as customer-owned abstract design.
## Rejection Conditions
Reject unverified attribution, image generation, private input, or scope escape.
## Open Questions
Which text format best preserves accessibility and reviewability?
