# Video Brief

Smoke-test example only. This document is documentation-only and must not be used as a final render instruction without human review and approval.

## Project

- Working title: Why AI agents need human approval gates
- Format: 60-second vertical short video
- Aspect ratio: 9:16
- Audience: Builders, operators, and technical decision-makers evaluating AI agent workflows
- Style: Clear, modern, slightly cinematic
- Source boundaries: Use only generic concepts and placeholder visuals. Do not use private, client, family, legal, medical, financial, or confidential material.

## Goal

Explain that AI agents can move quickly, but high-impact actions still need human approval gates to prevent accidental exposure, unsafe changes, or irreversible actions.

## Script Outline

### 0-5s Hook

Narration: "AI agents are fast. That is exactly why they need approval gates."

On-screen text: "Speed needs checkpoints"

### 5-18s Setup

Narration: "An agent can read, plan, edit, test, and prepare a handoff. But speed without review can turn a useful assistant into a risky operator."

On-screen text: "Read. Plan. Edit. Test. Handoff."

### 18-35s Core Idea

Narration: "Approval gates pause the workflow before sensitive reads, production commands, publishing, deployment, or anything that could expose private data."

On-screen text: "Pause before impact"

### 35-50s Practical Pattern

Narration: "The best systems make approval visible: what will happen, what files are involved, what risks exist, and what the human is approving."

On-screen text: "Show the action. Show the risk."

### 50-60s Close

Narration: "The future is not agents replacing judgment. It is agents doing the work, with humans keeping the final say."

On-screen text: "Agents act. Humans approve."
