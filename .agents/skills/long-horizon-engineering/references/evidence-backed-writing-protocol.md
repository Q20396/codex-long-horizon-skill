# Evidence-Backed Writing Protocol

Use this protocol for research notes, technical reports, academic-style writing,
claim-heavy documentation, reviewer responses, and public explanations.

## Core Rule

Every important claim should have evidence, a confidence level, or an explicit
uncertainty note.

## Workflow

1. Define section intent.
2. List the claims each section needs to support.
3. Attach evidence to each claim.
4. Separate facts, interpretation, recommendations, and speculation.
5. Check whether the evidence actually supports the wording.
6. Review from a skeptical reviewer mindset.
7. Run a pre-submission review before finalizing.

## Reviewer Mindset

Ask:

- Is the claim stronger than the evidence?
- Are terms defined?
- Are limitations visible?
- Are sources current and appropriate?
- Could a reviewer reproduce or verify the conclusion?

## Safety

Do not invent sources, quotes, dates, authors, laws, standards, or results. For
legal, medical, financial, security, or regulated claims, prefer official or
primary sources and keep advice informational unless a qualified professional is
involved.
